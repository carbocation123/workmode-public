# Introduction (teaching draft)

Calcination temperature is a very important parameter for oxide catalysts, and researchers have proved that 450 C always produces the best catalyst. It changes crystallinity, surface area and defect chemistry, so the catalytic activity will of course become better at a medium temperature. [citation needed]

CO oxidation is often used as a probe reaction for oxide surface reactivity. The concentration of oxygen vacancies is directly equal to the catalytic activity, and therefore a lower light-off temperature proves that more oxygen vacancies are present. [citation needed]

In this teaching study, samples C300, C450 and C600 were calcined at 300, 450 and 600 C, respectively. Preliminary activity files suggest that C450 reaches 50% conversion first. This result completely confirms that C450 contains the largest oxygen-vacancy concentration and reveals the reaction mechanism.
